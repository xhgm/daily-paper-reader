## 摘要
尽管模型容量和数据获取在持续扩展，视觉-语言-动作（VLA）模型在接触密集型和动态操作任务中仍然表现脆弱，微小的执行偏差可能会累积并导致失败。虽然强化学习（RL）为提升鲁棒性提供了一条原则性的路径，但物理世界中的同策略（on-policy）强化学习受限于安全风险、硬件成本和环境重置。为了弥补这一差距，我们提出了 RISE，这是一个通过想象进行机器人强化学习的可扩展框架。其核心是一个组合式世界模型，它（i）通过可控动力学模型预测多视角未来，（ii）通过进度价值模型评估想象的结果，为策略改进生成信息丰富的优势函数。这种组合式设计允许状态和价值由最合适但不同的架构和目标进行定制。这些组件被集成到一个闭环自我改进流水线中，该流水线在想象空间中持续生成想象轨迹、估计优势并更新策略，而无需昂贵的物理交互。在三项具有挑战性的现实世界任务中，RISE 相比现有技术取得了显著改进，在动态积木分拣、背包打包和纸箱关闭任务中，绝对性能分别提升了 +35% 以上、+45% 和 +35%。

## Abstract
Despite the sustained scaling on model capacity and data acquisition, Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models remain brittle in contact-rich and dynamic manipulation tasks, where minor execution deviations can compound into failures. While reinforcement learning (RL) offers a principled path to robustness, on-policy RL in the physical world is constrained by safety risk, hardware cost, and environment reset. To bridge this gap, we present RISE, a scalable framework of robotic reinforcement learning via imagination. At its core is a Compositional World Model that (i) predicts multi-view future via a controllable dynamics model, and (ii) evaluates imagined outcomes with a progress value model, producing informative advantages for the policy improvement. Such compositional design allows state and value to be tailored by best-suited yet distinct architectures and objectives. These components are integrated into a closed-loop self-improving pipeline that continuously generates imaginary rollouts, estimates advantages, and updates the policy in imaginary space without costly physical interaction. Across three challenging real-world tasks, RISE yields significant improvement over prior art, with more than +35% absolute performance increase in dynamic brick sorting, +45% for backpack packing, and +35% for box closing, respectively.
